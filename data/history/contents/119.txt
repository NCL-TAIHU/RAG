This thesis is designed to study Father Russell R. Sprinkle, a priest of Maryknoll mission from Catholic Foreign Mission Society of America. After Communist Revolution of 1949 in China, many foreign missionaries left mainland China for Taiwan. While the United States government provided aid to Taiwan, U.S Catholic Relief Service of Taiwan was functional in dealing with the aid project.
Father Russell R. Sprinkle was assigned to Taiwan in 1953. While preached among Taiwanese people, he also studied Chinese traditional culture, and gradually identified with both Chinese and Taiwanese people. Catholic history does not receive much attention in Taiwan, this thesis is pioneer in the study of Catholic contribution in Taiwan.
